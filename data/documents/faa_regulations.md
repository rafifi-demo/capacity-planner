# Federal Aviation Administration
## 14 CFR Part 121 - Operating Requirements
### Relevant Excerpts for Cargo Operations

**Reference Document for Zava Operations**
**Last Updated: January 2026**

## Subpart P - Aircraft Dispatcher Qualifications and Duty Time

### 121.463 Aircraft Dispatcher Duty Time Limitations

No certificate holder may allow an aircraft dispatcher to remain on duty
for more than 10 consecutive hours unless the dispatcher has had an
opportunity for at least an 8-hour rest period during or immediately
preceding that time.

## Subpart Q - Flight Time Limitations and Rest Requirements

### 121.471 Flight Time Limitations - Domestic Operations

(a) No certificate holder conducting domestic operations may schedule any
flight crewmember and no flight crewmember may accept an assignment for
flight time in scheduled air transportation or in other commercial flying
if that crewmember's total flight time in all commercial flying will exceed:
1. 1,000 hours in any calendar year
2. 100 hours in any calendar month
3. 30 hours in any 7 consecutive days

(b) No certificate holder conducting domestic operations may schedule any
flight crewmember for flight deck duty for more than 8 hours during any
24 consecutive hours.

### 121.473 Flight Time Limitations - International Operations

(a) No certificate holder conducting international operations may schedule
any flight crewmember for flight time in scheduled air transportation or
in other commercial flying if that crewmember's total flight time will exceed:
1. 1,000 hours in any 12-calendar-month period
2. 100 hours in any calendar month
3. 32 hours in any 7 consecutive days

(b) Except as provided in paragraph (c), no certificate holder may schedule
a flight crewmember for flight deck duty for more than 12 hours during any
24 consecutive hours.

### 121.480 Rest Requirements - Augmented Crews

When a flight operation requires augmented crew:
- Flight time limit may be extended beyond 8 hours
- 8-12 hours: Minimum 3 pilots required
- Beyond 12 hours: Minimum 4 pilots required
- Rest facility must meet Class 1 or Class 2 standards

## Subpart T - Flight Operations

### 121.533 Responsibility for Operational Control

(a) Each certificate holder is responsible for operational control of each
flight it conducts.

### 121.535 Dispatch Release

No pilot-in-command may begin a flight unless they have received a dispatch
release from the certificate holder's operations control center.

### 121.537 Fuel Requirements

(a) No person may dispatch or release an airplane for a flight unless it
has enough fuel:
1. To fly to and land at the airport to which it is dispatched
2. Thereafter, to fly to and land at the most distant alternate airport
   specified in the dispatch release
3. Thereafter, to fly for 45 minutes at normal cruise consumption

## Subpart U - Aircraft Certification Requirements

### 121.153 Aircraft Requirements

(a) Each aircraft used by a certificate holder for supplemental operations
must be equipped with the required navigation and communication equipment.

(b) Cargo aircraft must meet fire suppression requirements in 14 CFR 25.857.
All freight compartments must be Class C or higher for commercial operations.

### 121.159 Cargo Stowage

(a) All cargo must be properly stowed and secured to prevent shifting
during flight.

(b) Cargo must not interfere with the proper function of any:
1. Exit
2. Control
3. Required equipment

## Part 91 - General Operating Rules

### 91.167 Fuel Requirements for Flight in IFR Conditions

No person may operate a civil aircraft in IFR conditions unless it carries
enough fuel to:
1. Complete the flight to the first airport of intended landing
2. Fly from that airport to the alternate airport
3. Fly after that for 45 minutes at normal cruising speed

## Cargo-Specific Regulations

### Dangerous Goods (49 CFR 175)

Transportation of dangerous goods by air must comply with:
- Proper classification and identification
- Appropriate packaging and labeling
- Required documentation (Shipper's Declaration)
- Notification to pilot-in-command
- Quantity limitations per package and aircraft

### Known Shipper Requirements (49 CFR 1548)

All cargo must be:
- From known shippers (validated identity)
- Properly screened per TSA requirements
- Documented in the chain of custody

---

## International Requirements (ICAO)

For international operations, additional requirements include:
- ICAO Annex 6 compliance
- Bilateral air service agreements
- Foreign operator permits
- Customs and immigration documentation
