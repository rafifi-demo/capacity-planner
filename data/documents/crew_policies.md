# Zava Global Logistics
## Crew Resource Management Policy
### Flight Crew Scheduling and Duty Time Guidelines

**Policy Number:** OPS-CRM-2026-001
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Next Review:** July 1, 2026

---

## Section 1: Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes crew scheduling guidelines that exceed FAA minimum
requirements to ensure safety, crew well-being, and operational efficiency.

### 1.1 Applicability

This policy applies to:
- All flight crew members (Captains, First Officers, Flight Engineers)
- All operations from Zava hubs
- Both domestic and international flights

---

## Section 2: Duty Time Limitations

### 2.1 Basic Duty Limits

Zava duty limitations (exceeding FAA minimums):

| Operation Type | Maximum Duty Period | FAA Minimum |
|---------------|---------------------|-------------|
| Domestic | 10 hours | 8-9 hours |
| International | 12 hours | 10-12 hours |
| Augmented Crew | 16 hours | 12-16 hours |

### 2.2 Extended Duty Provisions

Extended duty may be authorized when:
- Operational necessity exists
- Crew has had adequate prior rest
- Cumulative fatigue factors are acceptable
- Weather and routing are favorable

Maximum extension: 2 hours beyond standard limit (with concurrence)

### 2.3 Monthly Flight Time Limits

| Period | Zava Limit | FAA Limit |
|--------|------------|-----------|
| 7 consecutive days | 30 hours | 30-32 hours |
| Calendar month | 85 hours | 100 hours |
| Calendar year | 900 hours | 1,000 hours |

---

## Section 3: Rest Requirements

### 3.1 Minimum Rest Periods

| Rest Type | Duration | Requirements |
|-----------|----------|--------------|
| Pre-duty rest | 10 hours | Opportunity for 8 hours sleep |
| Post-duty rest | 10 hours | Uninterrupted |
| Weekly rest | 36 consecutive hours | At domicile preferred |
| Monthly days off | Minimum 8 days | Non-consecutive acceptable |

### 3.2 Reduced Rest Provisions

Reduced rest (minimum 8 hours) may be used when:
- Next rest period is extended by equal reduction
- Not more than 3 reduced rest periods in 7 days
- Crew agrees to reduced rest
- Operations approves in writing

---

## Section 4: Augmented Crew Requirements

### 4.1 When Augmented Crew Required

Flights exceeding 8 hours require augmented crew:
- 8-12 hours: 3 pilots minimum (Heavy Crew)
- 12-16 hours: 4 pilots minimum (Double Crew)

### 4.2 Rest Facility Requirements

| Class | Description | Max Flight Time |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Class 1 | Bunk with privacy, horizontal | 16 hours |
| Class 2 | Lie-flat seat with privacy | 14 hours |
| Class 3 | Reclining seat | 12 hours |

### 4.3 Crew Composition - Augmented Flights

For flights requiring augmented crew:
- All pilots must be qualified in type
- At least 2 pilots must be Captain-qualified
- Relief pilots must complete full pre-flight briefing

---

## Section 5: Scheduling Policies

### 5.1 Schedule Publication

- Monthly schedules: Published by 15th of preceding month
- Changes: 72-hour notification minimum
- Reserve duty: 12-hour notification for call-out

### 5.2 Bid System

Crew scheduling uses seniority-based preferential bidding:
1. Captain bids processed first
2. First Officer bids processed second
3. Flight Engineer bids processed last
4. Open time assigned to reserves

### 5.3 Training Integration

Training events integrated with line flying:
- Annual recurrent: 3-4 days
- Proficiency checks: Integrated with line check
- New aircraft type: 3-4 weeks initial
- Upgrade training: Per program requirements

---

## Section 6: Fatigue Management

### 6.1 Fatigue Risk Management System (FRMS)

Zava operates under an approved FRMS including:
- Fatigue reporting system (confidential)
- Bio-mathematical modeling
- Workload analysis
- Continuous monitoring

### 6.2 Crew Responsibility

Flight crew members are responsible for:
- Arriving fit for duty
- Reporting fatigue concerns
- Using proper rest opportunities
- Self-assessing fitness before flight

### 6.3 Fatigue Call Procedures

If a crew member determines they are too fatigued to fly safely:
1. Notify Crew Scheduling immediately
2. Complete Fatigue Report form
3. Reserve crew will be activated
4. No punitive action for good-faith fatigue calls

---

## Section 7: Time Zone Considerations

### 7.1 Circadian Disruption

For flights crossing multiple time zones:
- +/- 4 time zones: Standard rest applies
- +/- 5-8 time zones: Extended rest required (12 hours)
- +/- 9+ time zones: Minimum 24-hour layover

### 7.2 Recovery Time

After eastbound/westbound rotations:
- Minimum 2 local nights before return to domicile duty
- Additional recovery for multiple rotation patterns

---

## Section 8: Special Operations

### 8.1 Night Operations

Night operations (2200-0600 local) considerations:
- Maximum 3 consecutive night departures
- Night premium applies per CBA
- Enhanced fatigue monitoring

### 8.2 High-Frequency Operations

For operations with multiple sectors:
- Maximum 4 sectors per duty day
- Sector time considerations in duty calculation
- Extended turnaround if delay occurs

---

## Section 9: Compliance and Reporting

### 9.1 Monitoring

Crew scheduling monitors:
- Individual cumulative duty/flight time
- Pattern compliance
- Rest period adequacy
- Exception tracking

### 9.2 Violation Reporting

Duty time exceedances must be:
- Reported to Chief Pilot within 24 hours
- Documented in crew member file
- Analyzed for systemic issues
- Reported to FAA if regulatory violation
